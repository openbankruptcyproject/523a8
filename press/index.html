Press & Research Inquiries

Single-page reference for journalists, academics, and policy researchers covering the Open Bankruptcy Project's empirical analysis of § 523(a)(8) student-loan-discharge adversary proceedings.

~61×

Annualized filing rate of § 523(a)(8) adversary proceedings increased ~61-fold after the November 17, 2022 DOJ/ED guidance. Recent (2015–2022) baseline: ~17 APs/year nationally. Post-guidance rate: ~1040 APs/year (annualized from the 17 months observed since Nov 2022).

What the data shows

  • The pre-DOJ baseline was flat for decades. Yearly § 523(a)(8) AP filings sat between 1 and 28 from 1991 through 2022. There was no upward trajectory before the guidance.
  • The DOJ guidance shifted the floor. 2023, 2024, 2025, and partial 2026 each individually exceed the entire decade preceding the guidance.
  • The shift was administrative, not legislative. 11 U.S.C. § 523(a)(8) was not amended. Brunner v. New York State HESC (1987) is still the controlling case in most circuits. The change was DOJ's internal procedure for stipulating to undue hardship.
  • Federal loans dominate the dataset. ~77% of post-guidance APs name the Department of Education as a defendant — consistent with the guidance's focus on federal loans.

Useful framings

"Twenty years of stable, low filing volume — then a 60-fold annualized rate jump in 17 months. Almost certainly the largest single procedural-administrative change in modern bankruptcy discharge access."
"What the data describes is a regulatory floor, not a regulatory ceiling. DOJ stopped contesting the marginal cases. The substantive law didn't move."
"Most public discussion of student-loan-discharge bankruptcy still references the pre-guidance era. The empirical reality has changed; the public framing hasn't caught up."

Background on the Open Bankruptcy Project

The Open Bankruptcy Project is a 501(c)(3) public charity (EIN 41-5159631; IRS Letter 947 issued April 2026) that builds open empirical infrastructure for federal bankruptcy court transparency. The project's prior empirical work has been cited by the Federal Rules of Bankruptcy Procedure Advisory Committee. Operations are volunteer-staffed and donation-funded, with no corporate sponsorship.

Press / academic inquiries: Contact via openbankruptcyproject.org/contact. We are happy to walk through methodology, share underlying data files, or discuss research collaborations.
