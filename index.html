§ 523(a)(8) Adversary Proceedings — Filing Patterns Pre vs Post DOJ Guidance

An open empirical dataset of 1,771 student-loan dischargeability adversary proceedings filed in U.S. bankruptcy courts. Pre-November-2022 DOJ guidance (n=254) compared to post-guidance (n=1,516).

1. Headline Finding: ~61× Annualized Rate Increase Post-DOJ-Guidance

The November 17, 2022 DOJ/Department of Education guidance reframed the attestation procedure for § 523(a)(8) adversary proceedings, materially lowering the procedural barrier to discharge. The annualized filing rate jumped roughly 61-fold:

PeriodTotal filingsYears observedFilings per year
Pre-DOJ (2015–2022, recent baseline)~1368~17
Post-DOJ (Nov 2022 – Apr 2026)1,516~1.5~1040

The full dataset covers § 523(a)(8) AP filings from 1991 through 2026, with 254 pre-guidance filings and 1,516 post-guidance filings — a raw 6.0× ratio. But the pre-guidance count is spread over more than three decades; comparing annualized rates against the recent (2015–2022) baseline yields the ~61× figure.

The 150 Stale-Abandoned Post-DOJ Cases

An additional finding: 150 post-guidance APs in the dataset have no termination date but no docket activity for 180+ days. Possible interpretations: settled outside court without a termination order, debtor abandoned the proceeding, or awaiting court action that has not come.

6. Methodology

Data source

Adversary proceeding records pulled from the RECAP archive (Free Law Project) via the CourtListener API. Search filtered to bankruptcy courts (court IDs ending in b) with case names containing v. and a defendant pattern matching student-loan creditors: Department of Education, NELNET, Mohela, Navient, Sallie Mae, Great Lakes Higher Education, ECMC, Pennsylvania Higher Education Assistance Agency, Edfinancial, Aidvantage.

Pre/post DOJ classification

Cutoff date: November 17, 2022, the publication date of the DOJ/ED revised process for attesting to undue hardship in § 523(a)(8) APs.

Outcome classification (heuristic)

  • pending: no termination date set; last filing within 180 days
  • stale_abandoned: no termination date; last filing more than 180 days ago
  • quick_dismiss: terminated within 30 days of filing
  • settled_or_dismissed: terminated within 30–180 days
  • litigated: terminated 180+ days after filing
Limitations. Outcome classes are based on temporal signals only, not on the content of dispositive orders. Whether a "litigated" case ended in discharge or denial is not yet classified in this dataset; resolving that requires either CourtListener premium access (for docket-entry text) or sampling-based document review. See the repository README for ongoing work.

Date range

Filings dated 1991-09-27 through 2026-04-27.
