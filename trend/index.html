§ 523(a)(8) AP Filing Trend, 2008–2026

Year-by-year volume of student-loan-dischargeability adversary proceedings filed in U.S. bankruptcy courts. The Nov 17, 2022 DOJ/ED guidance shows up immediately as a discontinuous jump — the largest single-year change in the 35-year history of the dataset.

Reading the chart: The vertical dashed line marks Nov 17, 2022 (the DOJ/ED guidance publication date). Pre-guidance bars (blue) show the relatively flat baseline of roughly 10–28 filings per year nationally during 2008–2022. Post-guidance bars (red) show the surge: 2023, 2024, 2025, and the partial 2026 each substantially exceed the entire decade preceding the guidance.

Methodology caveat

RECAP archive coverage of bankruptcy adversary proceedings is incomplete and uneven across districts and years. Earlier years (especially pre-2010) likely undercount actual filings because RECAP's coverage of older filings depends on community contributions of older PACER documents. The trend is robust to this caveat — the post-2022 surge is too large to be explained by coverage variation alone — but absolute year-by-year counts should be read as floor estimates.
